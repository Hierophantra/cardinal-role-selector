## Architectural assessment

### 1. Sidebar proportions

Your `260px / 960px` split is currently correct.

**Do not widen the content pane yet.**

A common failure in dashboard systems is overreacting to large monitors and allowing content to sprawl horizontally. Once reading width exceeds a certain threshold, cognition fragments:
- KPI relationships weaken
- eye travel increases
- scan rhythm breaks
- "dashboard" becomes "spreadsheet"

The 960px center-column creates intentional density.

Especially important because your app is:
- reflective
- evaluative
- conversational
- weekly

—not data-grid heavy.

You are building:
- **operational reflection software**, not:
- analytics infrastructure.

Different psychological geometry.

You can revisit adaptive widths later if real-world usage proves constraint too tight.

For now: **fixed max-width is wisdom.**

Because accountability is not about isolated weeks. **It is about rhythm over time.**

This is where the software could become genuinely differentiated from generic productivity tools.
